## What Fluxcord bridges

Fluxcord synchronizes the following content between Discord and Fluxer channels:

- **Messages** — text content, with mention and emoji conversion
- **Edits and deletions** — including bulk delete
- **Replies** — threaded replies rendered on both platforms
- **Embeds** — rich embeds converted across platform formats
- **Attachments** — files up to 25 MB forwarded automatically
- **Pins** — pinned message state kept in sync
- **Voice** *(alpha)* — voice channel audio bridged via LiveKit runners

## How it works

<Steps>
  <Step title="Deploy the bot">
    Run Fluxcord using Docker. You need a Discord bot token and a Fluxer bot token.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Invite to your servers">
    Invite the Discord bot to your Discord server and the Fluxer bot to your Fluxer community.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Set up a bridge">
    Run `fc!setup` in the channel you want to bridge on one platform, then confirm on the other.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Messages flow automatically">
    Fluxcord listens for events on both sides and forwards content through webhooks — no further action needed.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
  Fluxcord is self-hosted. You run the bot on your own infrastructure using Docker. There is no hosted version.
</Note>
